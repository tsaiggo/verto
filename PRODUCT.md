# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Verto serves serious individual readers first: engineers, researchers, founders,
students, writers, and creators who read in order to think, synthesize, and
build. They work across long-form documents, notes, feeds, and books, and need a
 calm, trustworthy workspace that keeps source material, annotations, reading
state, and AI output in one place without losing file ownership.

## Product Purpose
Verto is an AI-native personal knowledge reader: a local-friendly workspace that
turns MDX, Markdown, HTML, RSS, EPUB, and reading notes into one portable,
searchable, readable library. Its job is not to become a CMS or generic editor;
its job is to help people ingest, read, annotate, summarize, connect, and
revisit knowledge with AI while keeping every output grounded in the original
source.

## Brand Personality
Verto should feel calm, credible, and restrained. The product voice should read
like an expert tool for deep work: confident without bravado, polished without
ornament, and modern without chasing novelty. The interface should support
thinking, not compete for attention.

## Anti-references
Avoid generic SaaS dashboard aesthetics, decorative marketing gloss, and playful
UI noise that weakens reading focus. Avoid product surfaces that feel stitched
together feature-by-feature, where each page invents its own shell, spacing,
and interaction grammar. Avoid AI experiences that detach summaries, notes, or
chat from the source material they are supposed to help the user understand.

## Design Principles
1. Reading is a thinking workflow, not content inventory management.
2. Every AI action must stay grounded in the source and produce reusable
   knowledge, not disposable chat output.
3. Local-first portability and file ownership are product features, not
   implementation details.
4. One coherent shell, one interaction grammar, and one visual system should
   span the whole product.
5. The UI should earn trust through clarity, restraint, and predictable
   structure.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Ship to a WCAG AA baseline across the app. Preserve strong text contrast in both
themes, support keyboard navigation, visible focus states, and reduced-motion
alternatives, and avoid relying on color alone to communicate status. Deep-work
surfaces should stay readable for long sessions and resilient for users with
color-vision differences or motion sensitivity.
